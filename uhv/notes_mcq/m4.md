## Section A: Nature – Four Orders and Mutual Fulfilment

**1.** Nature is defined in the module as:

- A) The physical environment surrounding human settlements
- B) The collection of all the units — air, soil, water, plants, animals, humans, and even distant celestial bodies
- C) Only the living organisms on Earth
- D) The ecosystem managed by human beings

> **Answer: B**

---

**2.** All units in nature can be classified into how many orders?

- A) Two
- B) Three
- C) Four
- D) Five

> **Answer: C**

---

**3.** Which of the following is correctly matched to its order?

- A) Physical order — grass and plants
- B) Bio order — air, water and metal
- C) Animal order — human beings
- D) Physical order — air, water and metal; Bio order — grass, plants, trees

> **Answer: D**

---

**4.** The human order consists of:

- A) All mammals including humans
- B) Human beings only
- C) Human beings and higher animals
- D) Human beings and their created technology

> **Answer: B**

---

**5.** The soil-plant interaction described in the module is an example of mutual fulfilment between:

- A) Animal order and bio order
- B) Bio order and human order
- C) Physical order and bio order
- D) Animal order and physical order

> **Answer: C**

---

**6.** How do animals and birds contribute to the bio order (plants) in nature?

- A) By competing with plants for soil nutrients
- B) By spreading seeds and protecting plants from harmful insects and pests
- C) By consuming all available plant matter
- D) By blocking sunlight from reaching plants

> **Answer: B**

---

**7.** How do animals enrich the physical order (soil)?

- A) By consuming minerals directly from the soil
- B) Their dung and dead bodies act as manure making the soil fertile
- C) By digging burrows that aerate the soil excessively
- D) By transporting soil from one place to another

> **Answer: B**

---

**8.** Regarding the human order's current relationship with the other three orders, the module states that:

- A) Human beings are mutually fulfilling for all three orders
- B) Human beings only interact with the bio order
- C) Human beings are not only unfulfilling for the other three orders, but are dominating and exploiting them to the extent of global warming and climate change
- D) Human beings have no significant impact on the other orders

> **Answer: C**

---

**9.** According to our natural acceptance, we want to:

- A) Exploit the four orders for maximum physical facility
- B) Remain neutral toward the other three orders
- C) Enrich all four orders
- D) Dominate nature to ensure human survival

> **Answer: C**

---

**10.** What happens when human beings understand the mutual fulfilment among the four orders?

- A) They can continue exploiting nature more efficiently
- B) They can be fulfilling for all four orders
- C) They can eliminate the need for the animal order
- D) They can replace the bio order with technology

> **Answer: B**

---

## Section B: Self-regulation and Abundance in Nature

**11.** Self-regulation in a forest means:

- A) Human beings manage and control the population of each species
- B) Government authorities set limits on how many animals each species can have
- C) The proportion of soil, plants and animals of various species is naturally self-regulated without needing human regulation
- D) The strongest species dominate and control all others

> **Answer: C**

---

**12.** Which of the following is an example of self-regulation in nature given in the module?

- A) Humans managing wildlife reserves to prevent overpopulation
- B) It never happens that lions eat up all the deer or deer eat up all the grasses
- C) Scientists controlling the reproduction rates of species
- D) Government laws preventing deforestation

> **Answer: B**

---

**13.** With right understanding, a human being will be:

- A) Able to fully control nature and its processes
- B) Self-organised, in harmony within, and participating in the harmony of the larger order
- C) Independent of the other three orders of nature
- D) Able to accelerate the exploitation of nature more efficiently

> **Answer: B**

---

**14.** The correct sequence of abundance in nature from greatest to smallest quantity is:

- A) Human order >> Animal order >> Bio order >> Physical order
- B) Bio order >> Physical order >> Animal order >> Human order
- C) Physical order >> Bio order >> Animal order >> Human order
- D) Animal order >> Bio order >> Physical order >> Human order

> **Answer: C**

---

**15.** The abundance in nature implies that:

- A) Resources are limited and must be carefully rationed by humans
- B) The physical facility required for any order is already available in abundance since each preceding order is far greater in quantity
- C) Human beings must create more resources through technology

## Section C: Existence as Co-existence – Units in Space

- A) The physical universe that can be observed through instruments
- B) Whatever exists
- C) Only the material world visible to human senses
- D) The collection of all living organisms

> **Answer: B**

---

**17.** The two types of basic realities in existence are:

- A) Matter and energy
- B) Living things and non-living things
- C) Space and units
- D) Consciousness and material

> **Answer: C**

---

**18.** Which of the following correctly describes space?

- A) Space is limited in size and has definite activity
- B) Space is unlimited, all-pervading, and no-activity
- C) Space is a unit submerged within other units
- D) Space is temporary and changes with time

> **Answer: B**

---

**19.** Units, in comparison to space, are:

- A) Unlimited in size and no-activity
- B) Limited in size and are active (activity is always taking place in them)
- C) Unlimited in size and highly active
- D) Limited in activity and unlimited in size

> **Answer: B**

---

**20.** 'Submergence' of units in space means:

- A) Units sink below the surface of space
- B) Units are destroyed by space over time

- D) Units contain space within them, but are separate from it

## Section D: Three Properties of Units in Co-existence with Space

---

**22.** Being in co-existence with space, every unit is self-organised, meaning:

---

**23.** The third property of units being in co-existence with space is that they:

- A) Compete with other units for space
- B) Randomly collide with other units
- C) Recognise their relationship with every other unit in space and fulfil that relationship
- D) Absorb energy from space and dissipate it

---

**24.** Existence as co-existence is best described as:
