## Section A: Understanding the Human Being (Co-existence of Self and Body)

**1.** According to the module, a human being is best described as:

- A) Only a physical body with biological needs
- B) A co-existence of the Self ('I') and the Body
- C) A social animal driven by emotions
- D) A material entity with consciousness as a by-product

> **Answer: B**

---

**2.** The 'Self' in the context of human being refers to:

- A) The physical body visible to the eyes
- B) The brain and nervous system
- C) What we term as 'I' — the consciousness entity
- D) The sum of social roles a person plays

> **Answer: C**

---

**3.** The need of the Self ('I') is:

- A) Physical facility such as food and shelter
- B) Happiness (e.g., feeling of respect)
- C) Quantitative satisfaction
- D) Sensory pleasure

> **Answer: B**

---

**4.** The need of the Body is:

- A) Continuous happiness
- B) Right understanding
- C) Physical facility (e.g., food)
- D) Qualitative feelings

> **Answer: C**

---

**5.** Which of the following correctly differentiates the needs of the Self from the needs of the Body in terms of time?

- A) Both Self and Body have temporary needs
- B) Self has temporary needs; Body has continuous needs
- C) Self has continuous needs; Body has temporary needs
- D) Both Self and Body have continuous needs

> **Answer: C**

---

**6.** The need for food and clothing is described as __________ in nature, whereas the feeling of respect is __________ in nature.

- A) Qualitative; Quantitative
- B) Continuous; Temporary
- C) Quantitative; Qualitative
- D) Material; Spiritual

> **Answer: C**

---

**7.** The needs of the Self are fulfilled by:

- A) Physio-chemical things
- B) Right understanding and right feeling
- C) Physical labour and exercise
- D) Accumulation of physical facility

> **Answer: B**

---

**8.** The needs of the Body are fulfilled by:

- A) Right feeling and right understanding
- B) Education and self-exploration
- C) Physio-chemical things
- D) Qualitative consciousness activities

> **Answer: C**

---

**9.** The activities of the Self include:

- A) Eating, walking, and breathing
- B) Desire, thought, and expectation
- C) Recognising and fulfilling only
- D) Labour, exercise, and routine

> **Answer: B**

---

**10.** The activities of the Body (e.g., eating, walking) are described as:

- A) Continuous and qualitative
- B) Permanent and structured
- C) Temporary in time
- D) Guided by right understanding

> **Answer: C**

## Section C: Gross Misunderstanding and Its Consequences

**18.** The 'gross misunderstanding' referred to in the module is:

- A) Assuming that happiness comes from self-exploration
- B) Assuming the human being to be only the Body
- C) Prioritising the needs of the Self over the Body
- D) Believing that physical facility is limited

> **Answer: B**

---

**19.** When a person assumes they are only the Body, they tend to:

- A) Focus on right understanding and right feeling
- B) Try to fulfil all needs through physical facility
- C) Practise self-regulation and health
- D) Engage in self-exploration

> **Answer: B**

---

**20.** A consequence of overusing physical facility due to gross misunderstanding is:

- A) Achieving continuous happiness
- B) Balanced prosperity for all
- C) Exploitation of natural resources and human beings
- D) Fulfilment of the needs of the Self

> **Answer: C**

---

**21.** The diagram on 'Gross Misunderstanding' shows that equating the continuous need of the Self with unlimited physical facility leads to:

- A) Self-organisation and prosperity
- B) A feeling of deprivation and an accumulation loop
- C) Harmony between Self and Body
- D) Natural acceptance

> **Answer: B**

---

**22.** The way to come out of the gross misunderstanding is to:

- A) Accumulate more physical facility
- B) Suppress the needs of the Body
- C) Understand the Self as a conscious entity and Body as a material entity with separate needs
- D) Treat sensation as the primary source of happiness

> **Answer: C**
