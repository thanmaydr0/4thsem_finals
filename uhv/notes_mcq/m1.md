## Section A — Need & Guidelines for Value Education (Q1–Q12)

**1.** What are the two fundamental questions every human being must address for a happy, fulfilling life?

- A) What to earn? and How to earn?
- B) What to do? and How to do?
- C) What to study? and Where to study?
- D) What to think? and How to think?

> **Answer: B**

---

**2.** Value Education (VE) primarily addresses which question?

- A) How to do?
- B) Where to work?
- C) What to do?
- D) When to act?

> **Answer: C**

---

**3.** Skill Development (SD) primarily addresses which question?

- A) What to do?
- B) How to do?
- C) Why to do?
- D) What is valuable?

> **Answer: B**

---

**4.** Which of the following best describes the relationship between values and skills?

- A) Skills are more important than values
- B) Values are needed only after skills are developed
- C) Values and skills are complementary; values take priority
- D) Values and skills are completely independent of each other

> **Answer: C**

---

**5.** Which major problem does the current education system suffer from according to the notes?

- A) Lack of physical infrastructure
- B) Over-emphasis on values at the cost of skills
- C) Skill-biased education without a base of values
- D) Insufficient focus on science and technology

> **Answer: C**

---

**6.** Which of the following is NOT one of the guidelines for Value Education?

- A) Universal
- B) Competitive
- C) Rational
- D) Leading to Harmony

> **Answer: B**

---

**7.** The guideline "Universal" in Value Education implies that values should:

- A) Vary based on nationality and gender
- B) Be applicable to all human beings for all time and all places
- C) Be defined by religious scriptures
- D) Be taught only in universities

> **Answer: B**

---

**8.** The "Rational" guideline requires that Value Education must:

- A) Be based on blind beliefs and dogmas
- B) Appeal to reasoning and be open to questions
- C) Consist of a set of dos and don'ts
- D) Be accepted without questioning

> **Answer: B**

---

**9.** "Natural and Verifiable" as a guideline for Value Education means:

- A) It should be accepted as given by authorities
- B) It should be naturally acceptable and experientially verifiable
- C) It should be based on scientific experiments only
- D) It is merely an intellectual exercise

> **Answer: B**

---

**10.** The "All Encompassing" guideline requires Value Education to cover:

- A) Only the individual level of human life
- B) Only thought and behavior
- C) All dimensions (thought, behaviour, work, understanding) and all levels (individual, family, society, nature/existence)
- D) Society and nature levels only

> **Answer: C**

---

**11.** The ultimate aim of the guideline "Leading to Harmony" is to:

- A) Maximize physical facility for individuals
- B) Promote competition among human beings
- C) Promote harmony within the individual, among human beings, and with entire nature
- D) Ensure economic growth

> **Answer: C**

---

**12.** The value of any entity is defined as:

- A) Its monetary worth in the market
- B) Its participation in the larger order of which it is a part
- C) Its usefulness to an individual
- D) Its rarity

> **Answer: B**

## Section B — Human Aspirations & Basic Requirements (Q13–Q22)

- A) Happiness comes from good relationships alone

- A) Lack of physical facility

---

**16.** For animals, physical facility is:

- A) Necessary but not adequate

**17.** For human beings, which of the following is true regarding physical facility?

- A) It is necessary as well as adequate
- B) It is necessary but not adequate alone
