### 50 Multiple Choice Questions

## Section A: Family, Relationship and Nine Feelings

**1.** The family is described in the module as:

- A) A voluntary social contract between individuals
- B) The basic unit or building block of human organisation
- C) A group of people sharing physical facility
- D) An institution created by the state for social order

> **Answer: B**

---

**2.** According to the module, how many important aspects of relationship are explored?

- A) Two
- B) Three
- C) Four
- D) Nine

> **Answer: C**

---

**3.** Which of the following correctly states the first aspect of relationship?

- A) Relationship has to be constructed and maintained through effort
- B) Relationship is between the Body of one person and the Body of another
- C) Relationship is between one Self (I₁) and another Self (I₂)
- D) Relationship is only between people who share the same beliefs

> **Answer: C**

---

**4.** Feelings in a relationship reside in:

- A) The Body
- B) The physical facility shared between people
- C) The Self ('I')
- D) Society's norms and rules

> **Answer: C**

---

**5.** The feelings in a relationship are described as:

- A) Vague and subjective, varying from person to person
- B) Definite and recognisable
- C) Dependent entirely on the Body's sensations
- D) Created through repeated interaction over time

> **Answer: B**

---

**6.** How many feelings (values) are there in a human-human relationship?

- A) Four
- B) Five
- C) Seven
- D) Nine

> **Answer: D**

---

**7.** Which is the foundational value (feeling) in relationship?

- A) Love
- B) Respect
- C) Trust
- D) Affection

> **Answer: C**

---

**8.** Which is the complete value (feeling) in relationship?

- A) Gratitude
- B) Reverence
- C) Trust
- D) Love

> **Answer: D**

---

**9.** The correct order of the nine feelings in relationship (1st and 9th) is:

- A) Love (1st) and Trust (9th)
- B) Trust (1st) and Love (9th)
- C) Respect (1st) and Gratitude (9th)
- D) Affection (1st) and Glory (9th)

> **Answer: B**

---

**10.** The current problem in fulfilling relationships, as per the module, is due to:

- A) Lack of physical facility
- B) Assuming ourselves to be the Body and evaluating relationships on the basis of the Body rather than feelings
- C) Too many people living together in families
- D) Absence of legal frameworks for relationship

> **Answer: B**

---

## Section B: Trust – Intention vs Competence

**11.** Trust is defined in the module as:

- A) Believing everything the other says without question
- B) Being assured that the other intends to make me happy and prosperous
- C) Developing a strong bond through long-term interaction
- D) Sharing physical facility with the other person

> **Answer: B**

---

**12.** In the eight-statement analysis of trust, questions 1a to 4a relate to:

- A) Competence (ability)
- B) Behaviour and conduct
- C) Natural acceptance (intention)
- D) Physical facility

> **Answer: C**

---

**13.** In the eight-statement analysis of trust, questions 1b to 4b relate to:

- A) Natural acceptance (intention)
- B) Competence (ability)
- C) Feelings and emotions
- D) Preconditioning

> **Answer: B**

---

**14.** According to the trust analysis, the intention to make others happy is:

- A) Certain for everyone (tick mark) but competence to always do so is questionable
- B) Absent in most people
- C) Limited to close family members only
- D) Equal to competence in all human beings

> **Answer: A**

---

**15.** A common mistake in evaluating relationships today is:

- A) Evaluating oneself on the basis of competence and the other on the basis of intention
- B) Evaluating oneself on the basis of intention and the other on the basis of their lack of competence, leading to doubt about the other's intention
- C) Trusting everyone unconditionally regardless of behaviour
- D) Assuming everyone has low competence

> **Answer: B**

---

**16.** When we doubt another's intention based solely on their lack of competence, we develop:

- A) A feeling of affection
- B) A feeling of reverence
- C) A feeling of opposition instead of acceptance of the other as a relative
- D) Right understanding of the relationship

> **Answer: C**

---

**17.** If you trust the intention of the other and they are lacking competence, the right response is to:

- A) Get angry
- B) Get irritated and withdraw from the relationship
- C) Have a feeling of opposition
- D) Try to help improve their competence

> **Answer: D**

---

**18.** With the feeling of trust, one can see clearly that:

- A) Every person has a completely different intention
- B) Competence is the same for all people
- C) The intention (to be happy and make others happy) is the same for all; only the level of competence differs
- D) Strangers should never be trusted

> **Answer: C**

---

**19.** The module identifies which of the following as wrong assumptions reinforced by not separating intention from competence?

- A) Intentions are generally good in people
- B) "Strangers can't be trusted", "Trust is developed over a long time", "Never trust anyone"
- C) Competence can always be developed with effort
- D) Relationship is based on the Self, not the Body

> **Answer: B**

---

**20.** Trust on intention, as per the module, is:

- A) Blind trust that ignores competence
- B) The foundation of relationship and beginning of mutual development
- C) Conditional and depends on the other's track record
- D) Only possible between people who have known each other for years

> **Answer: B**

---

## Section C: Respect – Right Evaluation

**21.** Respect is defined in the module as:

- A) Giving more importance to people of higher status
- B) Following the rules of social etiquette
- C) Right evaluation
- D) Feeling of acceptance for excellence

> **Answer: C**

---

**22.** Disrespect can take place in how many ways?

- A) Two (over-evaluation and under-evaluation)
- B) Three (over-evaluation, under-evaluation, otherwise-evaluation)
- C) Four
- D) One (only discrimination)

> **Answer: B**

---

**23.** 'Otherwise evaluation' as a form of disrespect means:

- A) Evaluating a person for more than what they are
- B) Evaluating a person for less than what they are
- C) Evaluating a person for something other than what they actually are
- D) Refusing to evaluate a person at all

> **Answer: C**

---

**24.** The 'minimum content of respect' states that the other Self is similar to me in terms of:

- A) Body, wealth, and social status
- B) Physical strength, post, and beliefs
- C) Purpose, programme, and potential
- D) Competence, skills, and knowledge

> **Answer: C**

---

**25.** 'Our purpose is the same' in the context of respect means:

- A) We all have the same job and role in society
- B) We all have a natural acceptance to live with continuous happiness and prosperity
- C) We follow the same religious beliefs
- D) We belong to the same family

> **Answer: B**

---

**26.** The first set of differentiation that leads to disrespect is based on:

- A) Beliefs and ideologies (isms)
- B) Physical facility (wealth and post)
- C) Body characteristics (age, gender, race, physical strength)
- D) Education levels

> **Answer: C**

---

**27.** The second set of differentiation leading to disrespect is based on:

- A) Body characteristics
- B) Physical facility (wealth and post), based on the misunderstanding that physical facility = happiness
- C) Different religions and sects
- D) Level of education

> **Answer: B**

---

**28.** The third basis of differentiation that leads to disrespect is:

- A) Physical facility and wealth
- B) Age and gender differences
- C) Beliefs (isms, sects, different sets of information), based on the misunderstanding that matching preconditioning = being respectable
- D) Competence levels

> **Answer: C**

---

**29.** The 'complete content of respect' states that:

- A) The other is superior to me in all respects
- B) We are similar at the level of purpose, programme and potential, and complementary at the level of competence
- C) We must respect only those who have achieved excellence
- D) Respect is earned through demonstration of high competence

> **Answer: B**

---

**30.** Difference in competence, according to the module, should be seen as:

- A) A reason for discrimination and exploitation
- B) A justification for social hierarchies
- C) An opportunity to be complementary to each other
- D) A permanent gap that cannot be bridged

> **Answer: C**

## Section D: Other Nine Feelings – Affection to Love

**31.** Affection is defined as:

- A) The feeling of responsibility for the Body of a relative
- B) The feeling of acceptance for excellence
- C) The feeling of being related to the other (acceptance of the other as one's relative)
- D) The feeling of being related to everyone

> **Answer: C**

---

**32.** Lack of affection manifests as:

- A) Excessive care for physical needs
- B) Opposition and jealousy
- C) Reverence and gratitude
- D) Unconditional trust

> **Answer: B**

---

**33.** Care is defined as:

- A) The feeling of being related to the other
- B) The feeling of responsibility and commitment for ensuring right understanding in the Self of a relative
- C) The feeling of responsibility and commitment for nurturing and protection of the Body of one's relative
- D) The feeling of acceptance for those who have made effort for excellence

> **Answer: C**

---

**34.** Guidance is defined as:

- A) The feeling of responsibility for nurturing the Body of a relative
- B) The feeling of responsibility and commitment for development of Self by ensuring right understanding and right feeling in the Self of a relative
- C) The feeling of acceptance for excellence
- D) The feeling of gratitude for those who helped in one's growth

> **Answer: B**

---

**35.** Why does the module say our focus is mostly on Care rather than Guidance?

- A) Because Care is more important than Guidance
- B) Because we have assumed human being is only the Body, so we are not even aware of the Self
- C) Because Guidance requires more competence than Care
- D) Because physical facility is more important than feelings

> **Answer: B**

---

**36.** Reverence is:

- A) The feeling of being related to all
- B) The feeling of responsibility for the Body of a relative
- C) The feeling of acceptance for excellence
- D) The feeling of acceptance for those who made effort for my excellence

> **Answer: C**

---

**37.** Excellence, in the context of Reverence, is described as:

- A) High academic qualifications and professional achievements
- B) Being in a state of continuous happiness with completeness of understanding of harmony and living in harmony at all levels
- C) Having more physical facility than others
- D) Winning competitions and outperforming peers

> **Answer: B**

---

**38.** Glory is:

- A) The feeling of acceptance for those who have made effort for excellence
- B) The feeling of being related to everyone
- C) The feeling of acceptance for excellence itself
- D) The feeling of being responsible for the Body of a relative

> **Answer: A**

---

**39.** Gratitude is:

- A) The feeling of acceptance for those who have achieved excellence
- B) The feeling of acceptance for those who have made the effort for my excellence
- C) The feeling of being related to all people
- D) The feeling of responsibility for the development of a relative's Self

> **Answer: B**

---

**40.** The key difference between Love and Infatuation is:

- A) Love lasts longer while infatuation is a deeper emotion
- B) Love is based on sensation while infatuation is based on right understanding
- C) Love is based on being related to all and is not dependent on sensation; Infatuation is based on sensation and is conditional and temporary
- D) Love is only possible between family members while infatuation occurs between strangers

> **Answer: C**

## Section E: Justice and Human Goals

**41.** Justice is defined as:

- A) Following rules and laws set by the government
- B) Punishing those who violate social norms
- C) The recognition, fulfilment and evaluation of human-human relationship, leading to mutual happiness
- D) An equal distribution of physical facility

> **Answer: C**

---

**42.** The four comprehensive human goals articulated in the module are:

- A) Education, Health, Production, and Exchange
- B) Right Understanding (Happiness), Prosperity, Fearlessness (Trust), and Co-existence
- C) Trust, Respect, Affection, and Love
- D) Family, Society, Nation, and World

> **Answer: B**

---

**43.** The three obsessions in present-day society that arise from false assumptions are:

- A) Obsession for knowledge, power, and fame
- B) Obsession for consumption, profit, and sensual pleasure
- C) Obsession for justice, health, and education
- D) Obsession for love, trust, and respect

> **Answer: B**

---

**44.** Instead of fearlessness (trust), what prevails in today's society according to the module?

- A) Co-existence and mutual fulfilment
- B) Education and right understanding
- C) Domination, exploitation, and fear
- D) Prosperity and abundance

> **Answer: C**

---

**45.** The five basic systems (dimensions) of human order are:

- A) Family, Community, Village, Nation, World
- B) Trust, Respect, Justice, Preservation, Exchange
- C) Education-Sanskar, Health-Self regulation, Production-Work, Justice-Preservation, Exchange-Storage
- D) Happiness, Prosperity, Fearlessness, Co-existence, Harmony

> **Answer: C**

---

**46.** Education-Sanskar primarily leads to:

- A) Prosperity in every family
- B) Fearlessness and co-existence
- C) Right understanding and right feeling (happiness)
- D) Production of physical facility

> **Answer: C**

---

**47.** The two criteria for 'how to produce' in the Production-Work system are:

- A) Maximise output and minimise cost
- B) The process must be cyclic and mutually enriching (eco-friendly); and justice must be ensured with human beings (people-friendly)
- C) Use latest technology and increase efficiency
- D) Focus on profit and storage for future needs

> **Answer: B**

---

**48.** Preservation is defined as:

- A) Storage of physical facility for future consumption
- B) Saving resources for profit generation
- C) The recognition of relationship of human being with the rest of nature, its fulfilment and evaluation leading to mutual fulfilment (enrichment, protection and right utilisation of nature)
- D) Restricting usage of natural resources to zero

> **Answer: C**

---

**49.** Exchange in the context of the Exchange-Storage system means:

- A) Trade and commerce for profit maximization
- B) Sharing or exchanging physical facility with a view of mutual fulfilment, not with obsession for profit
- C) Exporting surplus goods to other nations
- D) A market-based mechanism for allocating resources

---

**50.** The correct sequence of units from smallest to largest in the human order is:

- A) World Family Order → Nation Order → Village Order → Family Order
- B) Family Order → Family Cluster Order → Village Order → Village Cluster Order → … → Nation Order → World Family Order
